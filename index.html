Numbers in IELTS

Your quick-reference FAQ — Listening · Reading · Writing · Speaking

Student Guide
🎧

Listening — What number types will I hear?

Q: What kinds of numbers appear?
AgeCurrencyDatesTimesMeasurementsPhone numbersCredit card numbers
Q: Where do numbers usually appear?
Most often in Part 1, but can appear in any of the 40 questions.
Q: Any spelling tip?
Write the numeral (e.g. 4.50) rather than spelling it out — avoids spelling errors.
🗣️

Speaking — How do I say numbers aloud?

Q: How do I say large numbers?
100,000 → "a hundred thousand"
3,500 → "three and a half thousand"
Q: How do I say years?
2020 → "two thousand and twenty" or "twenty twenty" — both are correct.
📖

Reading — How do I transfer number answers?

Q: What should I watch out for?
Copy numbers exactly as they appear — including commas, zeros, and currency symbols (e.g. $1,000 not 1000).
Q: Any common mistakes?
Dropping a zero in numbers with multiple zeros is very common. Double-check before moving on.
✍️

Writing Task 1 — Words or numerals?

Q: Can I mix words and numerals?
Yes — both forms are accepted:
10,000 or "ten thousand"
25% or "twenty-five percent"
75% or ¾
Q: What if exact numbers aren't shown?
Use approximation language:
just above 65% · under 70% · over half · almost 25%
Q: "Millions" vs "million" — what's the rule?
General → millions of dollars
Specific → 20 million dollars ✅ 20 millions dollars ✗
👂

Listening Trap — -teen vs -ty

Q: How do I tell 13 from 30?
Listen for syllable stress:
-teen (stressed last)-ty (stressed first)
thirTEENTHIRty
fourTEENFORty
fifTEENFIFty
sixTEENSIXty
sevenTEENSEVenty
eighTEENEIGHty
nineTEENNINEty
📅

Dates — What formats are accepted?

Q: How many ways can I write a date?
All of these are correct for 5 March:
March 5th · Mar 5th · 5th of Mar · 03/05 · 5/3
Q: What are the ordinal number exceptions?
Most end in -th, but watch these:
1st · 2nd · 3rd · 4th (NOT 2th ✗)
🕐

Time — How do I write it correctly?

Q: Are both digital and phrase forms accepted?
Yes:
6:45 = "six forty-five" or "quarter to seven"
6:15 = "six fifteen" or "quarter past six"
6:30 = "six thirty" or "half past six"
📞

Phone & Card Numbers — Special rules

Q: What does "O" mean in a number?
Zero (0) is sometimes said as the letter O. e.g. 03 → "O three"
Q: What are "double" and "triple"?
77 → "double seven"
333 → "triple three"
Example: 2450-7762-3338 → "…double seven…triple three…"
💡 Top tip for all four skills: Numbers only lose you marks when you rush. In Listening, write the numeral first and spell it out later if time allows. In Writing, always check your Y-axis — are you reading thousands or millions? In Reading, copy every digit and symbol exactly. In Speaking, practise saying large numbers and years aloud before test day.
